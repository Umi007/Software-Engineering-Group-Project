REDUCE YOUR ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT

INTRODUCTION

First things first, what is a Carbon Footprint?
It is an estimation of the amount of greenhouse gases your lifestyle produces, it can also be used for companies or products and anything else. There is emphasis on estimate, as a Carbon Footprint is challenging to calculate, because there are countless things to consider and it will never be 100% accurate. Our calculator looks at four main aspects of someone's life, food, transport, purchasing, and energy.

The Greenhouse Effect


Greenhouse gases include carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide, and water vapor (which all occur naturally), and fluorinated gases (which are synthetic), they cause the Greenhouse Effect. Solar radiation is absorbed by the Earth and Infra-red radiation (heat) is emitted some escapes into space, but some is reflected by greenhouse gases which cause the planet to warm up. This is good to an extent as without the Greenhouse Effect we would all freeze, but it has increased due to human activities releasing more greenhouse gases leading to climate change. The climate is just the average weather for an area over time, if the world increased by only a couple degrees (which it already has) this could change the climate drastically and have very severe consequences. Already we are seeing more unpredictable weather, storms are become more intense and frequent destroying infrastructure, there are intense heatwaves leading to fires and droughts. These are only a few consequences, the world is in a fine balance any disruption leads to a multitude of cascading issues. Visit the useful links page to find out more.

Below are some lifestyle suggestions that could help reduce your impact on the environment, more specifically reduce the amount of greenhouse gases produced as a result of your actions. It is a hard thing to do, altering your habits, but if we all make small changes we can influence change. You can also campaign and support organisations that try to influence the government and companies to make change. Thank you for showing an interest, only if you choose to try one small step, it is a step in the right direction.

FOOD

  • Limit the amount of meat you consume. Meat takes much more greenhouse gases to produce than vegetables (It takes 27kgCO2e to produce 1kg of beef, compared to 2kgCO2e for 1kg of vegetables). Meat production is also responsible for a lot of deforestation so livestock can graze. You do not need to become vegan over night, perhaps eating more vegetarian meals and buying meat alternatives.
  • Buy more local and seasonal produce. Out of season food like having strawberries in winter, produces greenhouse gases from heating large industrial greenhouses or from importing overseas goods.
  • Try to only buy food you need to lower the amount you waste. Taking a shopping list to stop unnecessary buying, try to avoid overly packaged items or buy in bulk to reduce packaging, using your own bags.

    • CHALLENGE - For one day a week eat a vegetarian/vegan diet, here are some vegetarian recipes to get started or if you want to push yourself try a week.

TRANSPORT

  • Try to use more public transport where possible, or walking and cycling are even better. If you must drive, carpooling is better.
  • Driving smarter, so choosing to journey in off peak times or avoiding roads that are known to be congested. You can also follow driving etiquette and the law to keep your mpg high, which uses less Carbon Dioxide.
  • When buying a new car try to find more efficient cars, or think about hybrids and all electric vehicles. The government offer a plug-in-grant where they will pay for a portion of your new car if it classed as low emissions. There are over schemes available also.

    • CHALLENGE - Use public transport or walk/bike to work (or anywhere else) for one day a week, or the entire week to make a real difference.

PURCHASING

  • Do not buy unnecessary things, try to only buy things you really need and know that you will use.
  • Before throwing something in the bin think of ways to reuse or recycle the item. Clothes, books, etc. can be sent to charity shops. You can also buy second hand or recycled goods from the offset, which promotes the industry and is better for the environment.
  • Try to buy from fair trade and sustainable sources, and items with minimal recyclable packaging. Always look for high quality items that will last and not need updating every few months.

    • CHALLENGE - Next time you want some new clothes try visiting second hand shops, or exchanging.

ENERGY

  • There are many small changes you can make around the house, make sure to turn off appliances not just leaving it on stand by, buy energy efficient products, lower you heating by just one degree.
  • Reduce the amount of water you use, collecting rain water or use grey water for you garden, having less or shorter showers and not baths, only use a dish washer or washing machine when it is full and wash pots by hand if you can, turn the tap off when you clean your teeth.
  • You can make changes to your house as well, such as using LED lightbulbs which are more efficient longer lasting and cheaper than fluorescent bulbs, insulating your home, switching to a green energy provider, low flow fittings, double glazing, solar panels.

    • CHALLENGE - Try to not leave all your appliances on standby.
